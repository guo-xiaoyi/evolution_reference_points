Instructions Overview

In this experiment, you will see several compound lotteries They are one single lottery composing of several sub-lotteries expaned over time. . Your task is to provide a valuation to each of them in your first session When you participate in our experiment, it counts as a session. We have three sessions at all (so you will need to participate three times.) . After the first session, we will randomly pick one lottery and realize A realization of a lottery indicates you actually play this lottery and it has a result. it. At Session 2 and 3, you will valuate the rest of this lottery until it has been fully played. Note that you will have to evaluate 15 (fifteen) lotteries in this session, and the system will only pick one of the fifteen to realize. The following illustrates an examplary lottery:

The way of evaluating a lottery is very easy. We want to know how much unrisky cash would you like to receive to avoid playing this risky lottery. Hence, we will offer a series of money, and ask you to choose between accepting this fixed amount of money This means, you will accept this amount of cash that we offered, and exit this lottery. What happens to this lottery will not be influential to you. and staying in this lotteryThis means, you will decline the fixed amount of money we provide, and continue to play this lottery, your gain or loss will depend on how this lottery realizes according to the probability.. Please read this list row by row, up to below; each row represents a new offer proposed by us. The middle of each row illustrates our offer, which is a fixed amount of money. If you think you want to accept this offer, use your mouse to click the selection mark at the left; if you think you want to stay in this lottery, use your mouse to click the selection mark on the right. You will have chance to review your choice and change it. You can only choose one side for each offer. For example, the screenshot here shows this subject still wants to play this lottery until when we offer him/her CHF 2. When we offer the subject CHF 3, he/she will accept this offer and not want to play the lottery anymore.

To submitProceed to the next lottery., you have to provide your evaluation to each of the row (all offers we proposed.) You cannot submit until you made all decisions regarding our offers.

Please note that the decision you made here is irrelevant to your final payoff: You can consider yourself only providing insights of your valuation to us; you will neither really receive the money nor being expelled from playing the lottery by providing your evaluation to us.
