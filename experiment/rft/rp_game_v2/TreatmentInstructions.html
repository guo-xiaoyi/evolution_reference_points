Additional Payment Opportunity

Overview

In addition to the standard payments described earlier, you may be eligible for and additional payment that depends on your choices and luck

Important note: The determination of the additional payment is complex. It uses a sophisticated mechanism designed to ensure that you are paid in line with the preferences you express through your choices. If you do not fully understand how the additional payment is determined, this is not a problem — it is only important that you understand the task of evaluating the random payoff trees, and that you evaluate the random payoff trees as if they are real.

The additional payment process consists of three steps described below.

Step 1: Random Draw at the End of Session 1

At the end of Session 1, one of eight random payoff trees will be selected at random.

Of these eight payoff trees, two are eligible for an additional payment. You will be informed whether the selected payoff tree is one of these two. If the randomly selected tree is an eligible payoff tree, you will receive an additional payment at the end of Session 3. If you do not draw an eligible tree, you will still receive all standard payments as described before.

The eligible random payoff trees always involve gains only — you will never incur a loss from the eligible trees.

Step 2: Number Generator at the End of Session 3 (if eligible)

If you draw an eligible tree in Step 1, then you will use a number generator at the end of Session 3. The generator returns 1, 2, or 3. These correspond respectively to die outcomes 1-2, 3-4, and 5-6, and determine which version of your payoff tree will be used for the additional payment:

  1. Generator shows 1: This corresponds to die outcomes 1 or 2. The payoff tree from Session 1 will be used to determine the payment. This includes all three periods following Session 1. This corresponds to what you will have evaluated in Session 1.
  2. Generator shows 2: This corresponds to die outcomes 3 or 4. The remaining part of the tree from Session 2 will be used. This includes the two periods following Session 2. This corresponds to what you will have evaluated in Session 2.
  3. Generator shows 3: This corresponds to die outcomes 5 or 6. The remaining part of the tree from Session 3 will be used. This includes only the final period of the random payoff tree (three days after Session 3). This corresponds to what you will have evaluated in Session 3.

This payoff tree has realised outcomes of £31 in 3 days and £21 in 6 days (as these payoffs are three and six days after the evaluation of this remaining part of the tree in Session 2).
  3. Die shows 5 or 6: The remaining part of the tree from Session 3 is used. This includes only the final period of the random payoff tree. This corresponds to what you evaluated in Session 3. This payoff tree has a realised outcome of £21 in 3 days (as this payoff is three days after the evaluation of this remaining part of the tree in Session 3).

Step 3: Monetary Offer Comparison

In each evaluation task, you indicate which fixed amounts of money you would prefer over the payoffs of the random payoff tree. For the selected payoff tree version, we will randomly draw a monetary offer. We then compare this offer with your choices in the evaluation table from the corresponding session:

  1. If the offer is high enough for you to have indicated you would prefer such a fixed amount over the payoffs from the random payoff tree, you will receive the offered amount as your additional payment.
  2. If the offer is low enough for you to have indicated you would prefer the payoffs from the random payoff tree, you will receive the outcomes generated by that payoff tree as your additional payment (with the same time delays as when you evaluated the random payoff tree).

This additional payment does not affect your regular payments for participating in this study.
