Additional Payment Opportunities

You can see this page because you have been selected for a group with potential additional payments.

Overview

In addition to the standard payments described earlier, you may be eligible for additional payments depending on your choices and chance. This page explains how these additional payments work.

Note: Not all participants receive this opportunity — you have been randomly selected for this group. The additional payment process involves three steps described below.

Step 1 — Draw at the End of Session 1

At the end of Session 1, one of your eight random payoff trees will be randomly drawn. You will be informed whether this drawn tree is an eligible tree.

There are two eligible trees among the eight. If you draw an eligible tree, you will be eligible for additional payments at the end of Session 3. If you do not draw an eligible tree, you will still receive all standard payments as described before.

Important: The eligible trees always involve gains only — you will never experience a loss from the eligible trees.

Step 2 — Die Roll at the End of Session 3 (if eligible)

If you drew an eligible tree in Step 1, then at the end of Session 3 you will roll a die. The outcome of the die determines which version of your payoff tree will be used for the additional payment:

  1. Die shows 1 or 2: The full payoff tree from Session 1 is used — this includes all three periods.
  2. Die shows 3 or 4: The remaining part of the tree from Session 2 is used — this includes periods 2 and 3, given the period 1 outcome already realised.
  3. Die shows 5 or 6: The remaining part of the tree from Session 3 is used — this includes only period 3, given the periods 1 and 2 outcomes already realised.

Each outcome has an equal probability of one-third (1/3).

Step 3 — Monetary Offer Comparison

For the selected payoff tree version, we will randomly draw a monetary offer within the range of possible outcomes of that tree version. We then compare this offer with your reported value in the evaluation table from the corresponding session:

  1. If the offer is higher than your reported value, you will receive the offered amount as your additional payment.
  2. If the offer is lower than or equal to your reported value, you will remain in the payoff tree and receive the outcome generated by that tree over time as your additional payment.

Incentive compatibility: It is in your best interest to report your true valuation of each payoff tree in the evaluation tables. The comparison mechanism ensures that truthful reporting gives you the best expected outcome.
