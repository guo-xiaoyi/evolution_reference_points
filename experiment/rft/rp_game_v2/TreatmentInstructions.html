Additional Payment Mechanism

An additional payment procedure is used to ensure that your payment reflects the preferences you express in your choices.

You do not need to understand all details of the payment procedure. It is only important that you evaluate each random payoff tree carefully and as if it were real.

The additional payment is determined in following steps.

Step 1 — Selection of Your Payoff Tree

In Session 1, you evaluate sixteen payoff trees in two sets.

After Session 1, one of these payoff trees from Set 2 will be randomly selected. It will be a tree with three time periods.

This selected tree is your payoff tree and is used in Sessions 2 and 3.

Step 2: Determination of Eligibility

Independently of the selection of your payoff tree, two out of eight payoff trees are designated as eligible for an additional payment.

If your selected payoff tree is among these two eligible trees, you will have the opportunity to receive an additional payment.

You will never experience a loss from this procedure, because the eligible trees only contain gains.

Step 3: Selection of the Relevant Tree Portion

If your payoff tree is eligible, a random number from 1 to 3 will determine which part of the tree is used for the additional payment at the end of Session 3:

  • Outcome 1: The full payoff tree from Session 1 is used.
  • Outcome 2: The remaining part of the tree from Session 2 is used.
  • Outcome 3: The remaining part of the tree from Session 3 is used.

Step 4: Payment Decision

For the selected version of the payoff tree:

  • A monetary amount within the range of possible payoffs of the relevant tree portion from Step 3 will be randomly drawn.
  • This amount will be compared with your choices in the evaluation table from the corresponding session.

If the monetary amount is high enough for you to have chosen the montary amount in the evaluation table, you will receive that amount.

Otherwise, you will receive the payments generated by the payoff tree (with the same delays as when you evaluated the payoff tree).
