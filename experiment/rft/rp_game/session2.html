Decision task

In today’s session, you will only evaluate one random payoff trees. Please find an example below.

The figure shows four columns, each representing a three-day period. The nodes and connecting lines show your random payoff tree, with a fortune wheel in the centre.

When a node has two outgoing lines, this means that one of two possible paths will be chosen by the fortune wheel. Once a path is chosen, the other path no longer matters.

The payoffs shown at the nodes add up over time. For example, if you reach the nodes labelled “-10” and “-12” and “+5”, your total loss over the entire time span will be £17.

How to evaluate a random payoff tree

Your main task in this experiment is to evaluate such random payoff trees. We want to know how much money such a random payoff tree would be worth to you. We will present you a series of alternative options and you will need to evaluate them one by one. When you click on “Start evaluation”, you will see a pop-out sidebar as the following:

You make a choice by clicking into a field in one row, and the rest will be filled automatically. For example: if you click on “I would prefer to receive £8”, then automatically all options where you receive more than £8 will also be selected (for comparisons where you would receive less than £8, the option “I would prefer to obtain the outcomes of the random payoff tree.” will then automatically be selected). If you click on another field, your choice will be updated. Once the indicated choices correspond to your preferences, click on “Submit”.

We will sometimes ask further about your choice. In this case, you can follow the same procedure, providing indicating your valuation in the table.

When you have submitted a selection, your answer is recorded and you cannot go back and change it. You will only evaluate one random payoff tree in this session!

The decisions that you make here do not determine your payment. You are only providing us with insights on how you are valuing the random payoff trees; you will neither participate in a random selected payoff tree nor receive or pay any amounts displayed in the tables where you indicate your choices.
